Apprenticeships for employers

What is an apprentice:

If you are an employer who wants to take on an apprentice, you should know the following:

  • apprentices are aged 16 or over (to any age)
  • they combine working with studying to gain skills and knowledge in a specific job
  • they can be new or current employees

Overview

You can get government funding to cover some of the cost of training and assessing an apprentice if you’re in England. Apprenticeship funding is devolved.

You need to pay the apprentice a salary that doesn’t go below the minimum wage.

Your apprentice must:

  • work with experienced staff
  • learn job-specific skills
  • study during their working week (for example, at a college or training organisation)

I want to find an apprentice for my business:

If you’re interested in taking on an apprentice you need to:

  • know how much it’s going to cost
  • which type of funding you can get
  • where to find an apprenticeship that suits your business needs
  • find a training provider who can deliver the right training
