How much will an apprenticeship cost me?

Funding your apprentice:

You can get help from the government to pay for apprenticeship training.

The amount you get depends on whether you pay the apprenticeship levy or not. You pay the levy if you’re an employer with a pay bill over £3 million each year.

Additional payments of £1,000 may be available to you and your training provider at the start of an apprenticeship. This will be paid (in 2 installments) when you take on an apprentice who’s between:

  • 16-18 years old
  • 19-24 years old and has previously been in care or who has a local authority education, health and care plan

From August 2018, apprentices will receive one payment of £1000 from the government, if they have been in the care of the local authority.

If you don’t need to pay the levy

You pay 10% towards the cost of training and assessing your apprentice.

You need to:

  • agree a payment schedule with the training organisation
  • pay them directly for the training

The government will pay the rest (90%) up to the funding band maximum. They’ll pay it directly to the training organisation.

If you pay the apprenticeship levy

You’ll receive funds to spend on training and assessing your apprentices. The government will add 10%.

Apprenticeship funding is devolved and so how you get your funds and pay for training depends on whether you’re in:

  • England
  • Scotland
  • Wales
  • Northern Ireland

Paying for your apprentices using a transfer of funds from another employer

If you’re an employer who’s received a transfer of funds from another employer, you can use these funds to pay for your apprenticeships.

You will receive your funds and pay for your apprenticeships using the apprenticeship service.
